The Data

This Graphic depicts the population growth of the Netherlands in relation to the immigration flow of Indonesians in a time period in which both countries have significant ties with eachoter. After the Second World War there was a high flow of immigration to the Netherlands, which largely consisted of immigrants from the former colony of the Dutch East Indies, now Indonesia. To read the visualization one should understand the history behind the data which will help the reader follow significant events depicted in the visualization. Read the explanation and see the events depicted per year in the data visuals.

it's History

On August 17, 1945, the proclamation of the Republic of Indonesia was by first president Sukarno. In 1946 there is a significant increase in migrants from Indonesia. In 1949 Queen Juliana signs the transfer of sovereignty to the United States of Indonesia.In 1950 the Royal Dutch-Indonesian Army (KNIL) was abolished and the Netherlands brought the former soldiers and their families to the Netherlands because of political tensions and promised the immigrants a short stay in the Netherlands. Most migrants strive for an independent Republic in the South Moluccas for their own, independent from Indonesia. They identify themselves with 'Moluccan' instead of Indonesian.The last boat containing old militants sails in 1953. The migrants will never be returned, which is the beginning of the Moluccan community in the Netherlands. Some immigrants still live in special communties and have up to 5 generations descendants.
